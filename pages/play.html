Protecting Every Child's Right to Safety

Every child deserves to grow up in a safe, nurturing environment free from abuse and violence

Understanding Child Protection

What is Child Abuse?

Child abuse includes physical, emotional, or sexual violence, neglect, and exploitation that results in actual or potential harm to a child's health, development, or dignity.

  • Physical abuse
  • Emotional abuse
  • Sexual abuse
  • Neglect

Risk Factors

Understanding risk factors helps in prevention and early intervention.

  • Social isolation
  • Family stress
  • Poverty
  • Lack of support systems

Child Rights

Every child has fundamental rights that must be protected.

  • Right to safety
  • Right to protection
  • Right to dignity
  • Right to be heard

Recognizing Signs of Abuse

Physical Signs

  • Unexplained injuries or bruises
  • Frequent injuries or accident-prone
  • Flinching when touched
  • Wearing inappropriate clothing to cover injuries

Behavioral Signs

  • Sudden changes in behavior
  • Withdrawal from friends or activities
  • Fear of certain places or people
  • Age-inappropriate sexual behavior

Emotional Signs

  • Low self-esteem
  • Depression or anxiety
  • Sudden mood swings
  • Excessive fear or worry

Social Signs

  • Poor peer relationships
  • Difficulty trusting others
  • Avoiding social interactions
  • Inappropriate social behavior

Protection Methods

Education & Awareness

Teaching children about personal safety and boundaries

  • Body safety rules
  • Safe and unsafe touches
  • Identifying trusted adults

Support Systems

Building strong support networks for children

  • Family support
  • School counseling
  • Community resources

Professional Help

Access to professional support services

  • Counseling services
  • Child protection services
  • Mental health support
